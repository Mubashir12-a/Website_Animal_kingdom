ANIMALS

Squirrel

Squirrels are generally small rodents with slender bodies, bushy tails and large eyes. In general, their fur is short, soft and silky, and ranges in thickness from species to species. The color of their fur is also highly variable and can be whitish, gray, yellow, red, brown, or even black.

Squirrels live in a variety of habitats around the world, but are not found in Antarctica, Greenland, extreme deserts, some island countries, or the southernmost part of South America.

Raccoons

Raccoons are medium-sized mammals with dense fur, a white face, and a ringed tail. They have a black facial mask over their eyes and their tails have 5â€“10 black bands.

Raccoons are native to North America and can be found in the United States, Canada, Mexico, and South America. They live in wooded areas and cities, and are often seen in human settlements looking for food.

Tiger

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest living member of the cat family, the Felidae. It feeds by hunting. It lives in Asia, mainly India, Bangladesh, Bhutan, Nepal, Myanmar, Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam, Malaysia, Indonesia, China, Korea and Siberian Russia. Tigers are solitary animals.

Tigers live in tropical rainforests, evergreen forests, temperate forests, mangrove swamps, grasslands, and savannas.

Cat

Cats are four-legged mammals with fur, soft paws, and attentive ears. They can be black, white, grey, orange, or a mix of these colors. Their fur protects them from the cold.

Cats are carnivores that hunt small animals in the wild. They use their sharp claws and teeth to kill prey.

Cats communicate through vocalizations like meowing, purring, trilling, hissing, growling, and grunting. They also use body language.

PLANTS

ZZ plant

The ZZ plant (*Zamioculcas zamiifolia*) is a popular houseplant known for its glossy, dark green leaves and resilience. It can tolerate low light, infrequent watering, and neglect, making it perfect for beginners. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that store water, helping it survive in dry conditions.

ZZ plants are also air-purifying and help remove toxins like xylene and toluene from indoor spaces. They're slow-growing but can reach up to 3 feet in height. Despite their hardiness, overwatering can lead to root rot, so it's best to let the soil dry out between waterings.

Snake Plant

The Snake Plant, also known as *Sansevieria* or *Mother-in-Law's Tongue*, is a hardy, low-maintenance indoor plant known for its upright, sword-like leaves that can vary in color from green to yellow-edged. It is an excellent choice for beginners due to its ability to survive in low light and minimal watering.

Snake plants are also known for their air-purifying properties, as they help remove toxins like formaldehyde and benzene from the air. They're drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of conditions, making them ideal for homes and offices.

Aloa

The Aloe plant, commonly known as Aloe Vera, is a succulent that has thick, fleshy leaves containing a gel-like substance. It is well-known for its medicinal and cosmetic properties, particularly for its soothing, healing, and moisturizing effects. Aloe Vera gel is often used to treat minor burns, wounds, and skin irritations. The plant is also used in skincare products, beverages, and traditional medicine.

Aloe Vera is easy to grow and requires minimal care, making it a popular houseplant. It thrives in dry, warm climates and does not need frequent watering.

Monstera

Monstera is a popular tropical plant known for its large, glossy, and uniquely split leaves. It is often called the "Swiss Cheese Plant" because of the natural holes and slits that develop in its leaves as it matures. Native to the rainforests of Central and South America, Monstera is a climbing plant that can grow very large under the right conditions.

Monstera is a popular indoor plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance. It thrives in indirect sunlight and requires moderate watering. The plant can also help improve indoor air quality by filtering toxins from the air.

INSECTS

Ant

Ants are social insects that live in well-organized colonies, often consisting of a queen, worker ants, and males. The queen's primary role is reproduction, while worker ants gather food, care for the queen, and maintain the nest. Ants communicate using pheromones, which help them create food trails, signal danger, or guide others. They're also incredibly strong, with some species capable of carrying objects many times their body weight.

Beetle

Beetles are insects with tough outer wings that protect their flying wings. Found in various environments, they have diverse diets, including plants, decaying matter, and other insects. Beetles undergo complete metamorphosis, moving through egg, larva, pupa, and adult stages. They play important roles in ecosystems by decomposing material, pollinating plants, and controlling pests.

Butter-Fly

Butterflies are colorful, flying insects known for their beauty and transformation. They go through four life stages: egg, larva (caterpillar), pupa (chrysalis), and adult. Butterflies feed on nectar from flowers and are important pollinators. They are found in various habitats and are a key part of ecosystems, helping plants reproduce and serving as food for other animals.

Fly

Flies are small, fast insects with a single pair of wings, belonging to the order *Diptera*. They are found almost everywhere and reproduce rapidly. Flies feed on various substances like decaying organic matter and sugary liquids. Some species, like houseflies, can spread diseases, while others, like fruit flies, are useful in scientific research.

PARROT

A parrot is a brightly colored, intelligent bird known for its ability to mimic sounds, including human speech. Parrots have strong, curved beaks, often used to crack nuts and seeds. Native to tropical regions, they are social, playful birds, commonly kept as pets due to their interactive nature.

EAGLE

An eagle is a large, powerful bird of prey with keen eyesight, sharp talons, and a hooked beak. Eagles are excellent hunters, soaring at high altitudes while scanning for prey. They are found in various habitats, including mountains, forests, and coastlines, and symbolize strength and freedom.

SPARROW

A sparrow is a small, adaptable bird commonly found in urban and rural areas. With brown and gray plumage, sparrows are social creatures known for their cheerful chirping. They often build nests in trees, shrubs, or buildings and feed on seeds, insects, and small plants in groups.

WOODPECKERS

A woodpecker is a bird known for its unique ability to peck into trees with its strong beak in search of insects or to create nests. Their drumming sound is distinctive in forests. Woodpeckers have specialized claws and stiff tails, helping them cling to tree trunks while foraging.
